Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data through deceptive emails, messages, or websites.

üîê

Password Security

Strong passwords are your first line of defense. Use unique, complex passwords for each account, enable two-factor authentication, and consider using a reputable password manager to keep your accounts secure.

üíæ

Malware Protection

Malware includes viruses, ransomware, spyware, and trojans that can damage your system or steal data. Keep your software updated, use reliable antivirus protection, and be cautious with downloads and email attachments.

üì±

Mobile Security

Mobile devices are increasingly targeted by cybercriminals. Keep your device updated, only download apps from official stores, use screen locks, and be cautious when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks.

üåê

Social Engineering

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. Attackers manipulate people into divulging confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

üîÑ

Data Backup

Regular backups protect against data loss from ransomware, hardware failure, or accidental deletion. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of important data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy stored offsite.

1
Think Before You Click

Always verify the sender and URL before clicking links or downloading attachments, especially from unexpected emails.

2
Keep Software Updated

Enable automatic updates for your operating system, browsers, and applications to patch security vulnerabilities.

3
Use Secure Networks

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities. If you must use it, consider using a VPN for added protection.

4
Monitor Your Accounts

Regularly check your bank statements, credit reports, and online accounts for suspicious activity.
