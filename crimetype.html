Phishing
Phishing is a deceptive practice where criminals send fake emails, text messages, or create fake websites that appear to be from legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

Common Examples:

  • Fake bank emails asking you to "verify" your account
  • Fake government notices demanding immediate action
  • Fake social media login pages
  • Email attachments containing malware
  • SMS messages with suspicious links

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Always verify sender's identity through official channels
  • Look for spelling and grammar mistakes in emails
  • Check URL carefully before entering sensitive information
  • Use multi-factor authentication
  • Keep antivirus software updated

Hacking
Hacking involves unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or devices to steal, modify, or destroy data. Hackers may target individuals, businesses, or government organizations for various motives including financial gain, political activism, or personal vendetta.

Types of Hacking:

  • Password cracking and brute force attacks
  • SQL injection attacks on websites
  • Network intrusion and data theft
  • Social engineering attacks
  • Man-in-the-middle attacks

Protection Strategies:

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • Enable firewall protection
  • Regular software updates and patches
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
  • Use VPN for secure browsing

Identity Theft
Identity theft occurs when someone unlawfully obtains and uses another person's personal information, such as social security numbers, credit card details, or other identifying information, typically for financial gain or to commit fraud.

Common Methods:

  • Stealing personal documents or mail
  • Data breaches exposing personal information
  • Skimming devices on ATMs or card readers
  • Social media information gathering
  • Phone scams requesting personal details

Prevention Methods:

  • Monitor credit reports regularly
  • Shred sensitive documents before disposal
  • Limit personal information on social media
  • Use secure payment methods online
  • Be cautious with personal information sharing

Cyber Bullying
Cyberbullying involves the use of digital technologies to harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person. It can occur through social media, messaging platforms, gaming platforms, or any digital communication channel.

Forms of Cyberbullying:

  • Sending threatening or hurtful messages
  • Posting embarrassing photos or videos
  • Creating fake profiles to impersonate someone
  • Excluding someone from online groups
  • Spreading rumors or false information

What You Can Do:

  • Block and report bullies on social platforms
  • Save evidence of cyberbullying incidents
  • Talk to trusted adults or authorities
  • Use privacy settings on social media
  • Support others who are being bullied

Ransomware Attacks
Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment in exchange for the decryption key. These attacks can target individuals, businesses, hospitals, and even government agencies, causing significant disruption and financial loss.

How Ransomware Spreads:

  • Email attachments with malicious code
  • Infected websites and downloads
  • USB drives and removable media
  • Network vulnerabilities
  • Remote desktop protocol attacks

Protection Measures:

  • Regular data backups (offline storage)
  • Keep software and OS updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Avoid suspicious email attachments
  • Implement network security measures

Online Scams & Frauds
Online fraud encompasses various deceptive schemes designed to trick people into giving money or personal information. These scams have evolved with technology and social media, becoming increasingly sophisticated and convincing.

Popular Online Scams:

  • Fake investment and cryptocurrency schemes
  • Romance scams on dating platforms
  • Fake lottery or prize notifications
  • Tech support scams
  • Fake online shopping websites

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Promises of easy money or guaranteed returns
  • Urgency tactics ("limited time offer")
  • Requests for upfront payments
  • Poor grammar and spelling in communications
  • Unsolicited contact from unknown parties
